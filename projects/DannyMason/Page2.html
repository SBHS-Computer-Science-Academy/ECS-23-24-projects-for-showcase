Bachelors Degree

Job Average Annual Salaray or Payment Per Hour Primary Duties Job Outlook
Project Manager $77,237 Annual Pay Project managers design, budget and implement projects in a variety of industries. They make sure deadlines are met, projects stay within budget and that team members are motivated. You’ll find project managers in almost every sector, and they are usually very knowledgeable in their industry. Although the BLS does not track specific job outlook data for project managers, openings for project management specialists and related positions are expected to grow 7% over the decade, with 70,400 total new openings.
Nuclear Engineer $80,686 Annual Pay Nuclear engineers research, design, operate and monitor nuclear equipment. They work in power plants, laboratories, offices and testing sites. They might work with medical equipment, nuclear power, research tools and emergency management equipment. Due to their wide need, nuclear engineers are found in a range of industries. Job openings are expected to decline 11% over the decade for nuclear engineers, though BLS still projects 700 job openings on average per year.
Petroleum Engineer $81,048 Annual Pay Petroleum engineers determine the best way to remove oil and natural gas from the earth. They gather and analyze data to find the safest and most cost-effective ways to drill, and design both the machines and systems used to extract petroleum. Petroleum engineers often work for oil companies, government agencies and oversight committees. Job openings are expected to grow 8% over the decade for petroleum engineers, or about 1,700 openings on average per year.
Chemical Engineer $81,153 Annual Pay Chemical engineers design, test and use systems and equipment for refining and combining raw materials to create important products. They often work with food and drugs but can work with any variety of chemicals. Chemical engineers are usually employed by laboratories, government agencies, food manufacturers and pharmaceutical companies. Job openings are expected to grow 14% over the decade for chemical engineers, or about 2,000 openings on average per year.
Construction Superintendent $82,183 Annual Pay Construction superintendents oversee construction sites. They are responsible for scheduling, safety compliance and supervising all tasks. Construction superintendents usually work for private construction firms. Job openings are expected to grow 8% over the decade for construction managers, or about 41,500 openings on average per year.
Architect $83,350 Annual Pay Architects design buildings. They determine their clients' needs, then draft, design and oversee the building of their project. They might work on residential, commercial, industrial or government buildings. Job openings are expected to grow 3% over the decade for architects, or about 9,100 openings on average per year.
Aerospace Engineer $84,345 Annual Pay Aerospace engineers design, build and test spacecraft, aircraft, missiles and national defense systems. They use advanced physics and aerodynamics to solve complex engineering problems and often work with the federal government, engineering firms and research facilities. Job openings are expected to grow 6% over the decade for aerospace engineers, or about 4,000 openings on average per yea
Statiscian $84,663 Annual Pay Statisticians gather, analyze and present numerical data to find trends and make predictions. They are useful in many industries and departments, including finance, human resources and marketing. Job openings are expected to grow 31% over the decade for statisticians and mathematicians, or about 4,100 openings on average per year.
Sales Engineer $86,002 Annual Pay Sales engineers sell technology solutions to companies that include upgrading systems, managing installed equipment and assisting in troubleshooting products. They must understand how their products work and how to meet user requirements. Sales engineers work closely with systems engineers and sales teams to meet their customers' needs and develop new products. Job openings are expected to grow 6% over the decade for sales engineers, or about 6,900 openings on average per year.
Compansation Manager $86,662 Annual Pay Compensation managers are in charge of developing and updating a company's payroll and pay structure. They must understand how to competitively pay and promote employees to recruit and retain workers. Compensation is important in every company and industry, so you’ll find compensation managers most everywhere. Job openings are expected to grow 2% over the decade for compensation and benefits managers, or about 1,200 openings on average per year.
Finance Manager $90,289 Annual Pay Finance managers analyze financial markets, advise on investments, submit financial reports and manage the financial health of their organization. Financial managers are needed in almost every industry. Job openings are expected to grow 17% over the decade for financial managers, or about 71,300 openings on average per year.
Database Administrator $91,037 Annual Pay Database administrators design, structure and update information databases so users can easily access the entries they need. Database administrators are also responsible for database security, and needed across several industries. Job openings are expected to grow 9% over the decade for database administrators, or about 11,500 openings on average per year.
Materials Engineer $91,095 Annual Pay Materials engineers work with a wide range of substances, such as metals, plastics and ceramics, to test and design materials to meet the needs of new products. They consider material costs, how quickly materials degrade and how to improve material quality. Materials engineers work in a variety of industries, firms and government agencies. Job openings are expected to grow 6% over the decade for materials engineers, or about 1,700 openings on average per year.
Information Security Analyst $92,774 Annual Pay Information security analysts search for weaknesses in computer networks, investigate security breaches and install and monitor security software. They are often consulted about software and systems upgrades and must stay up-to-date on computer viruses and malware. Information security is important to many industries and government agencies. Job openings are expected to grow 35% over the decade for information security analysts, or about 19,500 openings on average per year.
Software Engineer $94,062 Annual Pay Software engineers design, build and test computer operating system software and application software. They are well-trained in programming languages and design principles and create customized programs for their clients. Software engineers are in high demand in many industries and government agencies. Job openings are expected to grow 25% over the decade for software developers, quality assurance analysts and testers, or about 162,900 openings on average per year.
Ecominist $94,278 Annual Pay Economists analyze historical trends and product costs to solve problems and anticipate economic changes. They must have excellent data analysis skills and written and verbal communication skills. Economists work in many industries and often write articles. Job openings are expected to grow 6% over the decade for economists, or about 1,400 openings on average per year.
Electronics Engineer $95,038 Annual Pay Electronics engineers design, test and produce electronic equipment. This equipment can range from analog systems to microprocessors. Electronics engineers work in many different industries and often work in laboratories, manufacturing facilities and government agencies. Job openings are expected to grow 3% over the decade for electrical and electronics engineers, or about 20,100 openings on average per year.
Hardware Engineer $97,042 Annual Pay Hardware engineers design, build, test and manufacture physical components of technology. They understand how software and hardware interact and follow market trends to build appealing technologies. Hardware engineers work in research environments and with tech or computer companies, manufacturers and government agencies. Job openings are expected to grow 5% over the decade for computer hardware engineers, or about 5,300 openings on average per year.
Actuary $111,775 Actuaries use high levels of math, statistics and computer science to determine financial risks and find ways to manage such risks. They analyze data and trends to determine cost/benefit analyses and often work in insurance agencies or for the U.S. Social Security Administration. Job openings are expected to grow 21% over the decade for actuaries, or about 2,400 openings on average per year.
Network Architect $125,658 Annual Pay Network architects design network infrastructures that balance their companies' long-term technology goals with their financial needs. They are very experienced in computer science and understand how to translate business needs and goals into technological tools and systems. Network architects work in nearly every industry and government agency. Job openings are expected to grow 4% over the decade for computer network architects, or about 11,800 openings on average per year.
