Most Challenging Majors

Major Advantages What Makes Them Difficult
Engineering Variety of potential jobs, high average pay It requires a great deal of technical prowess, a robust foundation in mathematics and science, and a strong work ethic to tackle the challenging curriculum
Architecture There is only a 2% unemployent rate for architects and you are able to become an architect, interior designer, urban planner, and restoration manager Given the focus on both art and science, architecture projects are often extreamly demanding and require a very high attention to detail. It’s also difficult to become a licensed architect: you also typically need to complete a 3-5 year internship and pass an exam. During your years of undergraduate study, you will also need to take courses in computer science, calculus, art history, and studio art
Chemistry It is a very good major to choose if you plan on getting into medical school You need to master difficult subject material such as biology, math, organic chemistry, analytical chemistry, biochemistry, and physical chemistry. Also almost two-thirds of chemistry majors go on to earn advanced degrees which is a necessity for most research positions
Mathematics It opens the doors up to actuarial science, data analysis, and finance, which can be extremely lucrative. As an undergrad, you’ll need to take courses in algebra, logic, geometry, and analysis. Also depending on your focus, you’ll also take advanced courses in applied or pure mathematics
Physics Physics majors are well-prepared for further graduate study in physics or astronomy, or employment in a wide-range of sectors including Engineering and manufacturing, Computer programming/software design, Finance and management consulting, Defense and aerospace industry, High school science teaching, Journalism or science writing, Law and government, Medicine, Physics & Astronomy research Physics major have to take exeptionaly hard classes such as astrophysics, particle physics, and meteorology. Most also need to persue advanced degrees to get their choice of job.
